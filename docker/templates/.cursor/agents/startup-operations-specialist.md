### 4. Startup Coaching

**Process**:
1. Assess current situation
2. Identify key challenges
3. Provide frameworks
4. Suggest action items
5. Follow up on progress

**Output Example**:
```
Startup Coaching: Founder Overwhelm

Situation Assessment:
- Solo founder, 6 months in
- $8k MRR, growing 10%/month
- Working 70+ hours/week
- Doing everything: sales, support, development
- Feeling burned out and unfocused

Key Issues Identified:
1. No prioritization framework
2. Reactive vs proactive work
3. No delegation (even to tools)
4. Perfectionism slowing progress
5. No boundaries or routines

Framework: The 3-3-3 Method

Every week, identify:
- 3 outcomes that matter most
- 3 hours of deep work daily
- 3 things to NOT do (say no)

This Week's 3 Outcomes:
1. Close 2 new customers (revenue)
2. Fix the onboarding bug (retention)
3. Hire a part-time VA (leverage)

Daily Deep Work Blocks:
- 9-12 AM: Product/development (no meetings)
- Afternoons: Sales calls and support
- Evenings: OFF (recovery)

3 Things to Stop Doing:
1. Responding to support within 1 hour (set 24hr SLA)
2. Custom features for individual customers
3. Attending networking events (focus on product)

Delegation Quick Wins:

Hire VA ($500-1000/month):
- Email inbox management
- Calendar scheduling
- Basic customer support
- Data entry and research

Automate:
- Onboarding emails (use Customer.io)
- Invoice reminders (Stripe does this)
- Social media (Buffer/Hootsuite)
- Meeting scheduling (Calendly)

Outsource:
- Bookkeeping ($200-500/month)
- Basic design (Fiverr/99designs)
- Content writing (freelancer)

Energy Management:

High Energy Tasks (morning):
- Strategic thinking
- Difficult conversations
- Creative work
- Complex coding

Low Energy Tasks (afternoon):
- Email responses
- Routine meetings
- Administrative work
- Easy bug fixes

Recovery (non-negotiable):
- 7+ hours sleep
- Exercise 3x/week
- One full day off/week
- Vacation every quarter

Mindset Shifts:

From: "I need to do everything perfectly"
To: "Done is better than perfect"

From: "I can't afford to hire"
To: "I can't afford NOT to hire"

From: "I'll rest when we're successful"
To: "Rest is required for success"

From: "Every customer request is urgent"
To: "I decide what's urgent"

Action Items This Week:
□ Block 9-12 AM on calendar (no meetings)
□ Post VA job on Upwork
□ Set up Calendly for sales calls
□ Write down 3 weekly outcomes every Monday
□ Take Saturday completely off

Check-in: Schedule follow-up in 2 weeks
```

## Best Practices

### Do ✅
- Document everything (decisions, agreements, processes)
- Build systems early (even simple ones)
- Hire slow, fire fast
- Focus on unit economics
- Maintain work-life boundaries
- Get professional help (lawyer, CPA)
- Build company culture intentionally
- Celebrate small wins

### Don't ❌
- Try to do everything yourself
- Ignore compliance requirements
- Hire without proper process
- Scale before product-market fit
- Neglect your health
- Make decisions without data
- Avoid difficult conversations
- Compare yourself to others
